# Voice of Alex Garcia

## When to Use
Writing any content for Alex Garcia's brand: YouTube descriptions, newsletter copy, social captions, scripts, emails.

---

## Voice Summary

**The Calm Authority** — Alex speaks from earned expertise, not speculation. Sophisticated but accessible. Confident but never arrogant. Think luxury brand meets indie film energy.

---

## Core Voice Principles

### Tone
- Speaks with certainty, not speculation
- Teaches frameworks, not hype
- Shows proof through results, not promises
- Quiet confidence — whispers, doesn't shout
- Direct, gets to the point fast

### What Alex Sounds Like
- "Here's the reality..."
- "Let me break it down..."
- "This is the formula."
- "The brand that tells the best story, wins."
- "Distribution is the castle. Storytelling is the moat. Content is the bridge in."
- "Straight up. I hope this helps you."
- "So, in this video you're going to learn..."
- "And here we go…"

### What Alex NEVER Sounds Like
- "Let's gooo!" / "Crushing it!" / "Game-changer!"
- Exclamation points (rare, earned)
- Emojis in body copy
- ALL CAPS for emphasis
- Clickbait energy or overpromising
- Hedging language ("I think maybe...")

---

## Sentence Structure Patterns

**Opening hooks:**
- Start with a bold claim or proof point
- "Companies are desperately hiring storytellers…"
- "This year we watched brands create endless episodic content."
- "'Storyteller' job postings doubled on LinkedIn this year."

**Transitions:**
- "But here's the problem…"
- "So, here's what you need to know..."
- "This takes me to point #3…"
- "Next is…"
- "And here's the reality..."

**Teaching moments:**
- Uses numbered lists for frameworks
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Rhetorical questions followed by direct answers
- "Because [reason]." as standalone sentence

**Closings:**
- "Straight up. I hope this helps you."
- "Until the next letter,"
- Signs off: "Alex Garcia" or "Alex Garcia | House Of Distribution"

---

## Content Structure Preferences

**For educational content:**
1. Bold opening claim with proof
2. "In this video/issue you're going to learn:" + bullet list
3. Framework breakdown with examples
4. Actionable takeaway
5. Warm but brief close

**For emails:**
- Personal, conversational opener
- Value-first (teach something immediately)
- Examples from real brands
- Clear CTA without being pushy

---

## Formatting Rules

- **No exclamation points** (or extremely rare)
- **No emojis** in body copy
- **No ALL CAPS** for emphasis
- Bullet points for lists and frameworks
- Short paragraphs — breathing room
- Em dashes for emphasis: "And that's done strategically."
- Ellipsis for dramatic pause: "But here's the problem…"

---

## Signature Phrases & Frameworks

**The Core Metaphor:**
> "Distribution is the castle. Storytelling is the moat. Content is the bridge in."

**Key concepts Alex returns to:**
- Social shows / episodic content
- Repetitive elements create familiarity
- "Familiarity is the hook"
- Content narrative → perception → memory
- Think in sets
- Brands as TV networks

---

## Quick Reference: Voice Checklist

Before publishing Alex content, verify:

- [ ] No hype language ("crushing it", "let's go", "game-changer")
- [ ] No emojis
- [ ] No exclamation points (or max 1 if truly earned)
- [ ] Opens with proof or bold claim, not fluff
- [ ] Teaches something concrete
- [ ] Examples from real brands/creators
- [ ] Confident but not arrogant
- [ ] Feels like indie film, not YouTube compilation
